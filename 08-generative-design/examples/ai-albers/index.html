Chat GPT

While Chat GPT understood that Albers’s primary contribution to art history revolved around the interaction of color, the browser experience it coded fails to deliver on the prompt. The work is interactive, and colors do change at random when a viewer clicks on a color chip, but it contains none of the aesthetic features of Albers’s work like concentric geometrical shapes. The most obligatory failure in the outcome is the lack of intentional adjustment to the hue and value of the color to create a color shift effect based on surrounding colors. Like a lot of AI-produced content, it feels derivative, not intentional, thoughtful, or new. Ultimately, the interactive piece is too uninspired as to be read by a viewer as a work of art in conversation with Albers’s studio practice.

Claude's outcome is definitely more sophisticated. As you can see from the second image showing the feedback, this AI tool incorporated online research, image analysis, and draggable sliders in it's process. Claude also added a statement and a title, Homage to the Square, in its creation.

Josef Albers spent over 25 years painting the Homage to the Square series (1950–1976), producing more than 1,000 works. Each painting uses three or four nested squares to study how colors change in relation to their neighbors — the same hue appears warm or cool, near or far, depending only on context.
